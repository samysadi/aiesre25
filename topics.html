Energy Management and Storage with Bio-Based Phase Change Materials
By Daniel Cook

President - Conservation Solutions Corporation

www.conservationsolutions.com

Thermal Energy Storage presents a significant opportunity to reduce peak load energy demand and reduce the size of HVAC equipment. Reducing peak demand lowers the power requirements to maintain the grid, improve electric load and heating, cooling and process system reliability.

Other Phase Change Materials

Phase change materials such as ice have a limited range in which to optimize latent energy storage at 32°F and have issues with expansion and contraction shortening equipment life. Eutectic salts have a larger range in which to store latent energy but have issues with corrosion and a shorter operating life.

BioPCM Advantages

Bio-Based Phase Change Materials (BioPCMs) are renewably derived from vegetable oils and their by-products. They have a wide range of opportunities to set melt / freeze temperature from minus 70°C up to 175°C. They have minimal expansion and contraction issues with solid-to-gel, or solid-to-solid transitions. Latent heat of fusion varies from 150-275 kJ/kg.

1” of BioPCM stores as much thermal energy as 24” of concrete and has tunable thermal conductivity from 0.1-10 W/mK. BioPCMs are non-toxic and biodegradable with long term stability >100 years of thermal cycling with no thermal degradation.

Optimize Battery Energy Storage Efficiency

Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) are used as power protection systems for data centers (DC) and in large utility power distribution systems to provide back-up electricity during a power outage. During an outage when the batteries discharge, a large amount of heat is generated. The installed HVAC systems draw a significant part of the stored battery power to offset this generated heat. This reduces the power delivered downstream and reduces resiliency.

BioPCM systems have successfully offset approximately 41kW of heat generated by battery discharge over a 4-6 hour window and deliver the greatest amount of downstream electrical power using only 400 watts of power to maintain the shelter at 25°C with the BioPCM system during an event.

Hot and Chilled Water System Storage Opportunities

Other applications include thermal storage for solar water heating systems, geothermal systems, enabling hot water (boiler) and chilled water (Chiller) HVAC system peak demand to be reduced, and provide back-up heating and cooling when system go down as well as many other applications including agriculture, pharma and manufacturing.

Reduce Chiller or Boiler Short Cycling and Save Energy

Successful projects include reducing short cycling of chillers for a large manufacturer resulting in a 50% reduction in maintenance costs and 22% energy savings.

Domestic Hot Water Systems - Reduce Heat Pump Size

Storing large amounts of latent energy enables the thermal energy storage system footprint to be significantly reduced. BioPCM can be coupled with heat pumps to enable a 50% - 65% reduction in heat pump size for domestic hot water systems with a much smaller footprint than a water storage tank.

Portable Bio Based Phase Change Units

Portable Bio-based phase change systems are designed to satisfy the needs of a wide range of applications in the logistics industry, cold storage facilities and industrial applications weather it is heating or cooling to maintain reliable temperature control.

Cold Chain Logistics / Refrigerated Truck Transportation

Bio-based phase change systems can be installed in trucks to maintain desired temperatures to complete last-mile approach with sustainability at the forefront.

Challenges of Renewable Energy Integration in Clean Energy Systems
By Professor Frede Blaabjerg

IEEE Fellow and Villum Investigator
Leading Expert in Power Electronics and Renewable Energy Systems

Professor Frede Blaabjerg, IEEE Fellow (F'03), is a distinguished power electronics expert with a PhD in Electrical Engineering from Aalborg University (1995). He has held academic positions at Aalborg University, including Full Professor of Power Electronics and Drives, and has been a Villum Investigator since 2017. Blaabjerg's research interests span power electronics applications in wind turbines, PV systems, reliability, Power-2-X, power quality, and adjustable speed drives. With over 800 journal papers and ten monographs, he has received numerous accolades, including 46 IEEE Prize Paper Awards, the IEEE Edison Medal in 2020, and the Global Energy Prize in 2019. He served as Editor-in-Chief of IEEE Transactions on Power Electronics and has held leadership roles such as President of the IEEE Power Electronics Society (2019-2020). Blaabjerg was nominated by Thomson Reuters as one of the top 250 cited engineers globally (2014-2021).

Challenges of Renewable Energy Integration in Clean Energy Systems

In this talk, Professor Blaabjerg will address the complex transition from fossil-based energy to renewables. This shift necessitates highly efficient power electronics technology for energy conversion, distribution, and end-user applications. Professor Blaabjerg will focus on the role of advanced power electronics and control solutions in integrating renewable energies such as wind and photovoltaics into the grid, addressing issues of technology development, power converter technologies, system control, and synchronization. Additionally, he will explore future trends aimed at reducing energy costs and developing new power devices and tools to enhance system reliability, which are essential.

Energy Transition and Industry Decarbonisation: Emerging Pathways and Technologies
By Dr. Hacib BEN AISSA

Product Development Manager
Lynas Rare Earth Ltd., Malaysia

Dr. Hacib Benaissa's experience extends over 21 years and includes positions at different multinational companies like Shell, ExxonMobil, and Ceres Power. Energy Transition and Industry Decarbonisation: Emerging Pathways and Technologies

Dr. Benaissa will cover the role of low-carbon hydrogen and power-to-X technologies in industrial decarbonization. He will describe the integration of captured CO₂ from heavy industries with green or low-carbon hydrogen to reduce emissions. Dr. Benaissa, in his current role at Lynas Rare Earth Ltd., focuses on developing rare earth-based materials to support the hydrogen economy and CO₂ valorization.

Efficient Deep Learning for 3D/4D Shape Analysis: From Medical Applications to Generative AI
By Professor Hamid Laga

School of Information Technology
Murdoch University
Murdoch, WA, Australia

Hamid Laga is a professor with Murdoch University, Australia. His research interests span various fields of machine learning, computer vision, computer graphics, and pattern recognition, with a special focus on the 3D reconstruction, modeling, and analysis of static and deformable 3D objects, and on machine learning for agriculture and health. He is the recipient of the Best Paper Awards with SGP2017, DICTA2012, and SMI2006.

Efficient Deep Learning for 3D/4D Shape Analysis: From Medical Applications to Generative AI

The analysis of 3D and 4D (3D + time) shapes is crucial across diverse fields, from biology and medicine to computer graphics and virtual reality. Shape deformation over time is a key feature of many natural and man- made objects, such as anatomical structures, facial expressions, and human body movements. Understanding and modeling these deformations, along with the variability in shape, has applications in medical diagnosis, growth modeling, and animation. In this talk, Pr. Laga will describe his research on statistical modeling of static (3D) and dynamic (4D) shapes, focusing on the development of the Square Normal Fields (SRNF) framework for efficient 3D shape representation, comparison, and population modeling. The talk will also delve into statistical analysis of 4D shape data, particularly for applications in growth and disease progression, and highlight the role of these models in advancing deep learning and generative AI for 3D/4D reconstruction and generation from images, videos, and text.

Smart Battery and Hybrid Energy Storage Systems for Electric Vehicles and Drones: Advanced Energy Management Strategies and Applications
By Dr. Tedjani Mesbahi

Associate Professor (McF HdR)
INSA Strasbourg
ICube-CNRS laboratory, France

Dr. Tedjani Mesbahi is an Associate Professor at INSA Strasbourg, affiliated with the ICube-CNRS laboratory in France, specializing in electrical engineering and energy management. He holds an Engineer Diploma, M.Sc., Ph.D., and HdR, with extensive experience leading national and European research projects, securing over 10M€ in funding, managing 33 research projects and contributing to 2 technology transfer and start-up projects. Dr. Mesbahi has published over 120 papers, patents, and software licenses, earning recognition as one of the world's top 2% most influential researchers in electrical engineering, particularly in energy. His research focuses on optimizing energy management for electric vehicles, using AI to improve battery performance and longevity. Additionally, Dr. Mesbahi leads training programs in Industry 4.0 and coordinates research teams, contributing to the development of innovative solutions in the field.

Smart Battery and Hybrid Energy Storage Systems for Electric Vehicles and Drones: Advanced Energy Management Strategies and Applications

Dr. Mesbahi’s talk will explore the integration of smart battery systems and hybrid energy storage solutions, combining batteries and supercapacitors, for electric vehicles, drones, and domestic applications. He will focus on advanced energy management strategies using advanced control techniques and AI algorithms to optimize efficiency, predict the state of health and extend the lifespan of energy systems. A case study on the ELCOD project, which developed the Stork Mk.1 UAV, a long endurance hybrid-propulsion system combining a hydrogen fuel cell (PEMFC) and a LiPo battery is presented. This study highlights how hybridization can mitigate the durability challenges of hydrogen fuel cells by optimizing fuel cell sizing for cruising power and using complementary energy sources (e.g., batteries or supercapacitors) for peak loads. The talk also compares fixed and adaptive frequency-based energy management strategies and emphasizes the key role of supercapacitor state of charge in enhancing system performance and longevity in EV and drone applications.

Theme 1: Harnessing Renewable Energy: Advances and Innovations

Theme I explores the latest advancements in renewable energy technologies, focusing on innovations that enhance efficiency, sustainability, and integration across solar, wind, hydrokinetic, biomass, and geothermal systems, with a focus on cost-effectiveness where applicable.

  • Solar Energy: Examines innovations in photovoltaic (PV) and floating PV systems, highlighting their role in decentralized energy generation, environmental benefits, and economic viability.
  • Wind Energy: Focuses on advancements aimed at improving energy capture, grid integration, and cost reduction in wind energy technologies.
  • Hydrokinetic Energy: Discusses innovations that enhance energy extraction from water sources while minimizing ecological impact and associated costs.
  • Biomass Energy: Explores biomass conversion technologies and waste-to-energy strategies that support a circular economy, with a particular focus on their economic advantages.
  • Geothermal & Hydrothermal Energy: Reviews the current state of geothermal and hydrothermal technologies, their contributions to sustainable energy goals, and their role in grid stability and cost-effectiveness.

Topics Covered:

  • Solar Energy Systems
  • Wind Energy Systems
  • Hydro Energy Systems
  • Biomass Systems
  • Hydrothermal Energy
Theme 2: Innovations in Embedded Systems for Renewable Energy Management

Theme II focuses on the integration of renewable energy systems through advanced power electronics, monitoring technologies, and smart grid solutions. Key topics include innovations in power converters for efficient energy conversion, diagnostic techniques for improving system reliability, and methods for balancing supply and demand. Embedded systems play a vital role in optimizing renewable energy technologies, such as solar panels and wind turbines. These systems enable efficient data acquisition, real-time processing, and system monitoring to maximize the performance of renewable energy installations. Smart grid solutions, including advanced metering infrastructure (AMI) and demand response mechanisms, are essential for enhancing grid stability and resilience. Theme II will also explore the role of microgrids in local energy generation and community energy management. Discussions will focus on how microgrids can facilitate the integration of renewable energy at the local level and support resilient, decentralized power systems. Additionally, Theme II will assess the lifetime cost benefits of implementing embedded systems for renewable energy management, evaluating their long-term economic and environmental impacts to demonstrate their value in sustainable power system development.

Topics Covered:

  • Embedded Systems for Renewable Energy
  • Sustainable Power Systems
  • Microgrids and Smart grids
Theme 3: Green Computing (ML/AI) for Renewable Energy Integration

Theme III explores the intersection of computing efficiency and renewable energy integration, focusing on how Artificial Intelligence (AI) can enhance green computing by optimizing energy use and promoting sustainability. Key topics include energy-efficient algorithms designed to improve the performance and efficiency of renewable energy systems, AI-driven hardware that prioritizes energy efficiency and sustainability, and the application of predictive analytics for resource management. This includes forecasting energy production and consumption, optimizing energy storage, and balancing supply and demand. Theme III will also examine AI's role in smart grid integration, energy storage systems, and supporting a circular economy in the renewable energy sector, with an emphasis on innovations that minimize environmental impacts during the integration of renewable energy into the grid. Additionally, Theme III will explore how AI methodologies like Explainable AI (XAI) and Sustainable AI can ensure ethical, transparent, and sustainable energy solutions.

Topics Covered:

  • Artificial Intelligence for Renewable Energy
  • Explainable and Sustainable Artificial Intelligence
Theme 4: Advancing Hybrid Energy Systems for Sustainable Infrastructure

Theme IV explores hybrid energy systems, energy management, storage solutions, and hydrogen integration to enhance the stability and reliability of energy infrastructures. By combining renewable energy sources like solar and wind with energy storage systems, these hybrid systems can address variability in power generation, improve grid resilience, and increase overall system reliability, all while evaluating lifetime cost benefits and economic viability. Key areas of focus include optimal sizing and advanced control techniques for hybrid systems that balance renewable energy generation with storage, as well as energy management strategies that tackle the challenges of integrating renewables into weaker grids. The role of hydrogen in supporting sustainable energy systems is also highlighted, focusing on hydrogen production, storage, and its applications in decarbonization across various sectors. Additionally, Theme IV will examine the integration of advanced power electronics, real-time energy management, and hybrid energy storage technologies to optimize systems for sustainable infrastructure.

Topics Covered:

  • Hybrid Energy Systems: Design, optimization, and integration of renewable energy sources with storage technologies.
  • Biodiesel and Bioenergy Systems: Contributions to sustainable energy generation and biofuel production.
  • Energy Management and Storage: Optimization techniques for hybrid systems, including AI applications, and advanced hybrid energy storage technologies.
  • Heating and Cooling Systems: Energy-efficient solutions for residential, industrial, and commercial infrastructure.
  • Hydrogen Integration: Production, storage, and use of hydrogen as an energy carrier, including its role in decarbonization.
  • Electric Vehicles: Integration of electric vehicles with renewable energy systems and smart grids.
  • Energy Transport and Distribution: Technologies and strategies for efficient energy distribution across grids.
  • CO2 Reduction and Decarbonization: Technologies supporting carbon emissions reduction and the transition to a low-carbon energy future.
Theme 5: Energy Management and Storage

Theme V focuses on innovative solutions for energy management and storage, highlighting advancements in energy storage technologies such as batteries, fuel cells, and grid stability solutions. These technologies play a pivotal role in facilitating the integration of renewable energy and advancing a low-carbon energy future. A key focus is the transformative impact of Artificial Intelligence (AI) on Battery Management Systems (BMS). Theme V will explore how AI-driven optimization techniques enhance BMS, enabling smarter, more efficient grids, supporting renewable energy integration, and enabling autonomous microgrids. This results in improved efficiency, stability, and longevity of energy storage systems. Topics include AI-powered BMS, hybrid energy storage systems, smart grids, autonomous microgrids, and smart charging solutions for electric vehicles. Additional areas of interest include the development of new materials for energy storage, optimization strategies for storage systems, and innovations in energy transport and distribution. Theme V will also address challenges in battery modeling, state estimation, and the integration of renewable energy systems in achieving carbon-neutral communities. Furthermore, the role of machine learning in optimal cell balancing for EV batteries, cloud-based intelligent battery management, and hybrid storage technologies will be explored.

Topics Covered:

  • New Materials for Energy Storage
  • Optimization of Energy Storage systems
  • AI and Smart Connected Battery Management Systems (BMS).
  • Hybrid Energy Storage Technologies
  • Smart Charging for Electric Vehicles
  • Energy Transport and Distribution
  • Hydrogen Storage and Transport
  • Charging and Discharging Strategies
Theme 6: Innovations in Smart Agriculture and Sustainable Water Management

Theme VI explores innovative solutions at the intersection of smart agriculture and sustainable water management. It focuses on cutting-edge practices that optimize land use, enhance resource efficiency, and promote sustainability, particularly through the integration of renewable energy and advanced technologies. Key topics include agrovoltaics, which combine crop cultivation with photovoltaic technology to optimize land use for both food and energy production. The theme also covers solar water pumping systems, which improve irrigation and domestic water supply in off-grid or rural areas, addressing challenges related to water access and distribution. Additionally, the integration of biogas, wind energy, and other renewable sources in agricultural operations will be discussed as a means of enhancing energy security and reducing dependence on fossil fuels. Theme VI will also explore water prospecting techniques, such as geophysical surveys and remote sensing, to identify and manage water resources, particularly in arid or semi-arid regions. Other topics include water use optimization strategies like drip irrigation, rainwater harvesting, and precision farming, all aimed at improving water efficiency within the framework of Integrated Water Resources Management (IWRM). Smart farming techniques, including IoT-based monitoring and renewable energy-powered precision agriculture, will be highlighted for their potential to optimize water usage, improve crop yields, and conserve resources in arid climates. Theme VI will also address the financial, technological, and environmental barriers to implementing these solutions, emphasizing the importance of developing resilient agricultural practices that integrate food, water, and energy systems.

Topics Covered:

  • Smart Agriculture
  • Water Prospecting and Sustainable Management
  • Agrovoltaics and Dual Land Use
  • Solar Water Pumping Systems
  • Renewable Energy in Agriculture
  • Water Use Optimization (Drip Irrigation, Rainwater Harvesting)
  • Precision Agriculture and IoT-based Monitoring
  • Barriers to Technology Implementation in Agriculture
Panel Discussion: Renewable Energy Policy and Regulation, Education and Training, and Stakeholder Collaboration

This panel discussion will delve into how effective policies and regulations can stimulate investment in renewable energy technologies and infrastructure, driving economic growth and job creation. Key topics will include policies such as Feed-in Tariffs, Renewable Portfolio Standards, Net Metering, and Decarbonization strategies that incentivize investment and support sustainable energy production. The discussion will also emphasize the critical role of collaboration among government agencies, educational institutions, and industry stakeholders. These partnerships are essential to ensure that energy education and training programs are aligned with the evolving needs of the renewable energy market, addressing the growing skills gap in the sector. Panelists will explore the importance of community engagement in raising awareness of renewable energy systems and encouraging active participation in the energy transition. Education and training are fundamental in developing a skilled workforce that can drive decarbonization and the broader energy transition. The panel will explore how aligning educational systems with the evolving demands of energy transformation, particularly in countries like Algeria, can strengthen their ability to address climate change, create high-quality jobs, and build a sustainable energy future.

Focus Areas Covered:

  • Policy and Regulation in Renewable Energy
  • Energy Education and Training for the Future Workforce
  • Energy Transition and Decarbonization Strategies
  • Economic and Social Benefits of Renewable Energy
